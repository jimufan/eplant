Select an r-value cutoff range:


Use the slider to define a lower and upper r-value. The highest the r-value can be is 1, and that means that two vectors are a perfect match. Zero is no match, and -1 is a perfect anti-correlation, i.e. the expression response is exactly opposite to that of your gene of interest. The default lower r-value cutoff is 0.75, and the default upper r-value cutoff is 1.00. A tighter r-value range will result in smaller output files.

Choose the number of best (or worst) correlated genes you'd like to see.
